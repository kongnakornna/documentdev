**Managing Hotfix and Rollback When Deployment Fails**

When a new software deployment fails or causes issues, it is crucial to have a swift and effective recovery plan. Two primary strategies are commonly used:

**1. Hotfix**
   *   **Concept:** Releasing a small patch or fix to address a specific bug or issue discovered after deployment, without rolling back the entire change.
   *   **When to Use:** When the problem is well-scoped, can be fixed quickly, and applying the hotfix is less risky than a full rollback.
   *   **Process:**
        *   Identify and isolate the root cause.
        *   Develop and test the hotfix urgently (often in a separate branch or environment).
        *   Deploy the hotfix to the affected environment (typically via an abbreviated deployment pipeline).
        *   Thoroughly verify that the hotfix resolves the issue without side effects.

**2. Rollback**
   *   **Concept:** Reverting the system to the last known stable and working state prior to the recent deployment.
   *   **When to Use:** When the deployment has a critical failure (e.g., causes system outage, major errors, security issues) and an on-the-spot fix would be time-consuming or complex.
   *   **Process:**
        *   Stop user traffic to the new version or put the system in maintenance mode.
        *   Execute scripts or use tools to revert the code, database, and configurations back to the previous version.
        *   Verify that the system is fully operational in its former state.
        *   Analyze the root cause of the failure to improve the process for the next deployment.

**Best Practices:**
*   **Have a Prepared and Tested Rollback Plan:** Always design deployments to be easily and quickly reversible.
*   **Use Feature Flags:** Allow new features to be toggled on/off without a new deployment, providing better risk control.
*   **Implement Progressive Deployment (Canary, Blue-Green):** Reduce risk by releasing to a small subset of users first, enabling issue detection and informed decisions on whether to hotfix or rollback.
*   **Monitoring & Alerting:** Have systems in place to immediately detect problems post-deployment.
